## Starting Position
1. Lie flat on your back on a mat.
2. Keep your legs straight and together, arms at your sides or under your lower back for support.
3. Engage your core muscles.

## Execution
1. **Lift:** Slowly raise both legs towards the ceiling. Keep them straight and together.
2. **Hold:** Bring your legs to about a 45-degree angle. Maintain this position for a moment while keeping your core engaged.
3. **Lower:** Slowly lower your legs back towards the ground without letting them touch it. Stop just before the mat.
4. **Repeat:** Continue the motion for the desired duration.

## Tips for Proper Form
- Keep your back flat against the mat to avoid arching.
- Control the movement, avoiding using momentum.

- Focus on maintaining tension in your core throughout the exercise.

### Lie in Starting Position
Lie flat on your back with legs straight and together. Place your arms at your sides or under your lower back for support and engage your core muscles.

### Lift Your Legs
Slowly raise both straight legs toward the ceiling, keeping them together. Inhale as you lift and maintain core tension to protect your lower back.

### Hold at the Top
Bring your legs to approximately a 45-degree angle or higher while holding the contraction in your abs. Keep your back flat against the mat.

### Lower Without Touching
Slowly lower your legs back toward the ground, stopping just before your feet touch the mat. Exhale on the way down and repeat the motion under control.
