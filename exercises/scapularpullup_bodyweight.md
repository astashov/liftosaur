## Starting Position
1. **Grip**: Hang from a bar with a shoulder-width overhand grip.
2. **Arms**: Keep your arms fully straight with elbows locked.
3. **Hang**: Let your shoulders rise toward your ears in a relaxed dead hang.

## Movement
1. **Pulling Phase**:
   - Pull your shoulder blades down and slightly together.
   - Let your body rise a few inches while keeping your arms straight.

2. **Lowering Phase**:
   - Release the shoulder blades to let your body sink back into the dead hang.
   - Move only at the shoulder blades - keep the elbows straight throughout.

## Key Points
- Do not bend your elbows; the movement comes entirely from the shoulder blades.
- Keep the range small - only a few inches of body travel.
- Pause briefly at the top and feel your lats and lower traps engage.

### Hang From the Bar
Grab the bar with a shoulder-width overhand grip and hang with fully straight arms, letting your shoulders relax up toward your ears.

### Depress the Shoulder Blades
Pull your shoulder blades down and slightly together, raising your body a few inches. Keep your arms completely straight so the movement happens only at the shoulders.

### Pause at the Top
Hold the depressed position for a second and feel your lats and lower traps working.

### Return to the Hang
Slowly release the shoulder blades to sink back into the relaxed dead hang, then repeat without bending the elbows.
