## Setup
1. **Equipment**: Use a barbell and a sturdy bench or seat.
2. **Positioning**: Sit on the bench with your feet flat on the ground and shoulder-width apart.
3. **Barbell Placement**: Rest the barbell across your thighs, just above your knees, ensuring comfort.

## Movement
1. **Starting Position**: Keep your back straight and core engaged. Allow your heels to hang off the edge of the bench.
2. **Execution**:
   - Slowly raise your heels by pushing through the balls of your feet. 
   - Fully extend your ankles at the top of the movement.
3. **Lowering**: Gradually lower your heels back to the starting position, feeling a stretch in your calves.

## Tips for Proper Form
- **Control the Movement**: Avoid bouncing; use a smooth motion.
- **Alignment**: Keep your knees stationary and avoid shifting your body.
- **Foot Position**: Maintain a neutral foot position or vary by pointing toes in or out for different calf activation.

## Breathing
- Exhale while lifting your heels and inhale as you lower them. 

Ensure to focus on controlled movements for maximum effectiveness and safety.
