## Starting Position
1. **Hands**: Place your hands together under your chest so your thumbs and index fingers form a diamond shape.
2. **Feet**: Keep your feet together or slightly apart, maintaining a straight line from head to heels.
3. **Body**: Engage your core and keep your body straight.

## Movement
1. **Lowering Phase**:
   - Bend your elbows to lower your chest toward your hands.
   - Keep your elbows tucked close to your sides, not flaring out.
   - Lower until your chest nearly touches your hands.

2. **Raising Phase**:
   - Press through your palms to push your body back up to the starting position.
   - Drive through your triceps and keep your core tight throughout.

## Key Points
- Keep your elbows tucked close to your body to maximize tricep involvement.
- Avoid sagging your hips or piking your buttocks up.
- If full diamond push-ups are too hard, start from your knees.

### Set Up the Diamond
Place your hands together directly under your chest so your thumbs and index fingers form a diamond shape. Keep your feet together and your body in a straight line from head to heels.

### Lower Your Chest
Bend your elbows to lower your chest toward your hands, keeping your elbows tucked close to your sides rather than flaring out.

### Press Back Up
Push through your palms and drive through your triceps to return to the top, keeping your core tight and back straight.

### Scale the Difficulty
If full diamond push-ups are too hard, perform them from your knees until you build enough tricep strength.
