## Setup

1. **Equipment**: Use a resistance band, preferably a heavy duty one.
2. **Foot Position**: Stand on the band with feet shoulder-width apart. Position the band under the arch of your feet.
3. **Grip**: Bend down and grasp the band with both hands, keeping your arms shoulder-width apart.

## Form

1. **Starting Position**:
   - Keep your back straight and chest lifted.
   - Hinge at the hips, maintaining a slight bend in the knees.
   - Ensure your shoulders are directly above or slightly in front of the band.

2. **Lifting**:
   - Engage your core and push through your heels as you pull the band upward.
   - Straighten your hips and knees simultaneously.
   - Keep the band close to your body throughout the lift.

3. **Top Position**:
   - Stand tall with shoulders back and chest out.
   - Ensure a straight line from head to heels; avoid hyperextending the back.

4. **Lowering**:
   - Reverse the movement by hinging at the hips first.
   - Maintain control as you lower the band back to the starting position.
   - Keep your back neutral throughout the descent.

## Tips

- Focus on using your glutes and hamstrings during the lift.
- Avoid rounding your back; keep it straight.
- Ensure your knees do not cave in during the movement.

## Conclusion

The band deadlift is an excellent exercise for building strength in the posterior chain. Focus on maintaining proper form for maximum effectiveness and injury prevention.

### Set Up Your Position
Stand on the resistance band with feet shoulder-width apart, the band running under the arches of your feet. Bend down and grasp the band at shoulder-width with both hands.

### Establish the Starting Position
Hinge at the hips with a slight knee bend, keeping your back straight and chest lifted. Shoulders should be directly above or slightly in front of the band.

### Drive to Standing
Engage your core and push through your heels, straightening your hips and knees simultaneously. Keep the band close to your body throughout the lift and stand tall at the top.

### Lower with Control
Reverse the movement by hinging at the hips first, then bending your knees to return to the starting position. Maintain a neutral spine throughout the descent.
