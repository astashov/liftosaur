## Setup
1. **Equipment**: Use a triceps extension machine.
2. **Adjustment**: Set the seat height so your elbows align with the machine's pivot point.

## Starting Position
1. **Seat**: Sit back firmly against the pad with feet flat on the floor.
2. **Grip**: Grasp the handles with an overhand grip, elbows bent at roughly 90 degrees.
3. **Upper Arms**: Keep your upper arms parallel to the floor and stationary throughout.

## Execution
1. **Engage**: Brace your core and press your back into the pad.
2. **Extend**: Push the handles forward and down, fully extending your arms.
3. **Squeeze**: Pause briefly at full extension and squeeze the triceps.
4. **Return**: Slowly return to the starting position, maintaining control.

## Tips
- **Elbows Stable**: Keep your upper arms fixed — only your forearms should move.
- **Full Extension**: Lock out fully at the bottom to maximise triceps activation.
- **Avoid Momentum**: Use a slow, controlled tempo rather than bouncing through the movement.

## Breathing
- **Inhale** as you return to the starting position.
- **Exhale** as you extend through the movement.

## Common Mistakes
- Letting the elbows drift forward or upward during the extension.
- Using too much weight and losing control on the return.
- Not fully extending at the bottom of the movement.

### Set Up the Machine
Adjust the seat so your elbows align with the machine's pivot point. Sit back firmly against the pad and grip the handles with elbows bent at roughly 90 degrees.

### Brace and Keep Upper Arms Still
Press your back into the pad and brace your core. Your upper arms should remain stationary and parallel to the floor for the entire set.

### Extend Fully
Push the handles forward and down in a smooth arc until your arms are fully extended. Squeeze your triceps hard at the bottom of the movement.

### Return with Control
Slowly bring the handles back to the starting position. Inhale on the return and keep the weight under control — don't let it pull your arms back too fast.
