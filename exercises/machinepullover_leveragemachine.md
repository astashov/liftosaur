## Setup
1. **Equipment**: Use a pullover machine.
2. **Adjustment**: Set the seat height so your shoulders align with the machine's pivot point.

## Starting Position
1. **Seat**: Sit back firmly against the pad with feet flat on the floor.
2. **Grip**: Hold the bar or handles with an overhand grip, arms extended overhead.
3. **Pad Contact**: Press your elbows against the arm pads if available.

## Execution
1. **Engage**: Brace your core and retract your shoulder blades slightly.
2. **Pull Down**: Drive your elbows down and forward in an arc, pulling the bar toward your hips.
3. **Squeeze**: Pause briefly at the bottom and squeeze your lats.
4. **Return**: Slowly raise the bar back overhead to the starting position.

## Tips
- **Lead with Elbows**: Think of driving your elbows down, not pulling with your hands.
- **Full Stretch**: Allow a full stretch at the top to maximise lat engagement.
- **Stay Seated**: Keep your back pressed into the pad throughout — avoid lifting your hips.

## Breathing
- **Inhale** as you return to the starting position.
- **Exhale** as you pull down through the arc.

## Common Mistakes
- Shrugging the shoulders at the top of the movement.
- Using momentum rather than a controlled arc.
- Setting the seat too low, shifting stress off the lats.

### Set Up the Machine
Adjust the seat so your shoulders align with the machine's pivot axis. Sit back firmly and grip the handles or bar with arms extended overhead.

### Engage Before Pulling
Retract your shoulder blades slightly and brace your core. This keeps the movement focused on the lats rather than the lower back.

### Pull Down in an Arc
Drive your elbows down and forward in a smooth arc toward your hips. Think elbows first — not hands. Squeeze your lats at the bottom of the movement.

### Return with Control
Slowly guide the bar back overhead, allowing a full stretch at the top. Inhale on the way up and exhale as you pull down.
