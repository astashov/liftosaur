## Setup
1. **Equipment**: Use a cable machine set to chest height.
2. **Attachment**: Attach a single handle or rope to the cable.

## Starting Position
1. **Position**: Stand sideways to the cable machine, feet shoulder-width apart.
2. **Grip**: Hold the handle with both hands at chest level, elbows bent.
3. **Distance**: Step away from the machine until there is tension on the cable.

## Execution
1. **Engage Core**: Brace your core and glutes before initiating any movement.
2. **Press Out**: Extend both arms straight out in front of your chest, resisting the cable's pull to rotate you.
3. **Hold**: Pause for 1–2 seconds with arms fully extended.
4. **Return**: Pull the handle back to your chest in a controlled manner.

## Tips
- **Resist Rotation**: The goal is to prevent your torso from rotating — the challenge is the anti-rotation, not the pressing motion.
- **Stay Tall**: Keep your spine neutral and avoid leaning away from the machine.
- **Feet Stable**: Do not let your feet shift or your hips rotate during the press.

## Breathing
- **Inhale** as you return the handle to your chest.
- **Exhale** as you press out.

## Common Mistakes
- Allowing the torso to rotate toward the cable.
- Using too much weight, causing loss of core control.
- Letting the hips shift instead of staying square.

### Set Up the Cable at Chest Height
Attach a single handle to the cable machine at chest height. Stand sideways to the machine with feet shoulder-width apart, holding the handle with both hands at your chest.

### Brace Before You Press
Engage your core and glutes fully before moving. Your hips and shoulders should stay square throughout the entire set.

### Press Straight Out
Extend both arms directly in front of you at chest height. Resist the cable's rotational pull — the tension should be felt through your obliques and deep core, not your arms.

### Hold and Return
Pause with arms fully extended for 1–2 seconds, then return the handle to your chest with control. Complete all reps on one side before switching.
