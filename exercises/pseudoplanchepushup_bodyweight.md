## Starting Position
1. **Hands**: Place your hands by your waist, fingers pointing back or out to the sides.
2. **Feet**: Keep your feet together with a straight line from head to heels.
3. **Body**: Lean your shoulders forward so they sit in front of your hands, bracing your core with a slight posterior pelvic tilt.

## Movement
1. **Lowering Phase**:
   - Bend your elbows to lower your body while keeping the forward lean.
   - Keep your elbows close to your sides, not flaring out.
   - Maintain the shoulders-ahead-of-hands position throughout.

2. **Raising Phase**:
   - Press through your palms to push your body back up.
   - Protract your shoulder blades at the top and keep leaning forward.

## Key Points
- The forward lean is the whole point; standing too upright turns it back into a regular push-up.
- The farther forward your shoulders travel past your hands, the harder it gets.
- Keep your hips locked in line; do not let them drop or pike as your shoulders load.

### Set Up With a Forward Lean
Place your hands by your waist with fingers pointing back or out. Lean your shoulders forward so they sit in front of your wrists, keeping your core braced and your body in one line.

### Lower Under Control
Bend your elbows to lower your body while holding the forward lean, keeping your elbows close to your sides and your shoulders ahead of your hands.

### Press Back Up
Push through your palms to return to the top, protracting your shoulder blades and keeping the forward lean throughout.

### Increase the Lean
Shift your shoulders farther forward past your hands to make the movement harder as your straight-arm strength improves.
